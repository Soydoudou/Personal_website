Ersatz


This is a PC game named Ersatz. In the game, Ersatz is a trickster who aims to be the greatest thief of all time. However, he’s no run-of-the-mill burglar, as he has the power to rewind time! Players will play as Ersatz and infiltrate various locations in order to steal valuables. To do this, they will need to sneak around security and avoid capture. Thanks to Ersatz’s special power, they’ll be able to accomplish this by rewinding time to create clones that cause distractions and mayhem.

Intro

  • BACKGROUND

    CS3152 is a project-based course in which programmers and designers collaborate to make a computer game. This course investigates the theory and practice of developing computer games from a blend of technical, aesthetic, and cultural perspectives. Aesthetic and cultural include art and modeling, sound and music, game balance, and player experience.

  • DESIGN PHILOSOPHY

    Our design stems from our desire to create a single player cooperative stealth game. Most stealth games involve observing the guards from afar and identifying their patrol patterns; what makes our game unique is that the player is able to change and create patrol patterns by using Ersatz’s cloning powers. Players must counter-intuitively get the guards’ attention and lure them to another location. Our game will teach players a variety of unconventional techniques to maneuver their clones in ways to best interact with their future selves.

  • GAME CONCEPT

    Gamers play this PC product as a time traveling thief. For example, if a guard is in your way, you can distract the guard, go back in time, and send a separate copy of yourself to walk past the distracted guard.

Brainstorming and Evolution of the game concept
Originally we planned to make a standard stealth game. There were different types of enemies and the player needed to interact with different environmental objects such as doors and vents. The initial game had an action feel.
To make this game more fun, we changed the gameplay to be that the player could make copies of himself to lure enemies away so that the real player could pass by enemies. The game name Ersatz means “replacement” in German.
Our game was turned into a puzzle-stealth game with the copy feature. We therefore shifted our focus from creating more types of enemies and environmental objects to puzzle like level design. Each of us designed several levels(figure one the left: one of the levels I designed, which was a hard one).

Initial sketches
To give this game a sneaky feel, I drew a character with eye mask and a sack of stolen items. He has a big head because he was smart. He bends his body while walking because he doesn’t want to be discovered by any enemies.

Players' feedback
The game had too many environmental objects, which were confusing. To make the game play more intuitive, we only kept the versatile door and items for players to steal. Instead of making different types of enemies, we only kept the stationary type of enemy who would start to chase the player if he walked into the enemy’s line of sight.
