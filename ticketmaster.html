Ticket Master


Ticket Master demonstrates the interaction of transfering tickets for another game. This app is applicable to Italian leagues only.
(Note: My understanding of football was soccer. Also since FIFA is one of EA's most famous games, I thought I was asked to make a soccer ticket rebooking App and worked a lot on it . But after I reread the prompt again before I started to make this website, I realized that I might have been asked to make a football App. Tolu told me that I could continue the work. I kept working on it and finished the design of a soccer ticket rebooking App. I apologize for any misunderstanding!)

Earthquake Alert
Brandon is on his way to an exciting game: A.C.Milan VS Juventus. Suddenly, his phone makes an alarming sound. Ticket Master pops up an earthquake alert. "What?? It's cancelled?!!" Yelling hystericaly, Brandon is heart broken as the game he's been waiting for a long time is cancelled due to an annoying earthquake. But wait, it's not the end of the world. He's given the option to transfer his tickets for another game.

Search Matches by League
As a soccer loyal supporter, Brandon can always make time for soccer. So he does not need to search matches by date. Brandon is browsing the matches by league. He taps on the "Italian Serie A" tab and the "Coppa Italia" tab to see if there are any matches that would interest him.

Search Matches by Date
Similar to Brandon, Jack's game also got cancelled. He would love to go to another soccer match but he's on a very tight schedule. Therefore, he is browsing the matches by date. He might have some free time on January 23rd. So he searches the matches by date to see if there're any matches that would fit in his schedule.

Transfer Tickets By One Click
Both Brandon and Jack are directed to the ticket transfer page after they choose a match. On this page, they could see which sections are transferrable. After choosing a section, they are directed to the bottom of the page. They could choose to transfer all the tickets for the same game or for different games. Brandon's friend who was originally planning to go to the cancelled game with him told him that he might not want to go to other games. Brandon chooses to transfer only 1 ticket for the A.C. Milan VS Fiorentina game as he also wants to save the other ticket to see how A.C.Milan would perform in the match with other teams.

Confirmation
Congratulations! Brandon finally successfully transfers his ticket for the A.C.Milan VS Fiorentina game and he receives a $10 coupon for other games. The coupon is a compensation for cancelling the original game. He doesn't need to deal with customer representatives, wait in long line or having to browse every match to find the perfect one. He doesn't need to calculate how much extra he would pay if he accidentally buys a ticket much more expensive than the original ticket. And he gets a coupon for other games. A confirmation email is automatically sent to him. Brandon is much relieved. Now it's time to find the next perfect game as he still has one ticket!

Intro

  • SCENARIO

    A small earthquake is predicted to happen soon, and a game Brandon's attending is cancelled (adapted from the original prompt). Brandon uses Ticket Master to transfer his tickets for another game.

  • MY RESEARCH QUESTION

    How can a technology assist users conviniently and quickly transfer tickets for another game?

  • KEY GOAL

    Create a ticket rebooking app that is easy to use.

STEP1. OBSERVE

Conducted interviews with users who loved going to soccer games.
Interviews were conducted in person. The interviews were conducted in places where participants would use this app to transfer their tickets for another game. It's a good way to simulate the scenario.
Goal: I was interested in finding when/where/what/how match-goers interact with technologies.
Interview Questions
Basic information (demographic info): the purpose is to classify users based on their diversity.
1. Tell me about yourself. How old are you? What major are you/ what work do you do?
Reflections (scenario specific experiences): the purpose is to better understand users' needs by asking them to reflect on specific experiences.
1. How often do you go to soccer games? What was the last game you went to?
2. Have you ever encountered a situation that a game you were attending was cancelled and you needed to transfer your tickets for another game? What was the experience(when, where, who, what, why)? If this didn't happen to you please imagine how you would think in such a situation.
3. What were your thoughts when you were notified that a game was cancelled? What did you want to do?
4. Did you run into any trouble when transferring a game ticket? If so, what was it?
5. If a game is cancelled, what would be determinant for you to go to another game?
Technical Experiences (product specific experiences): the purpose is to better understand the context in which a soccer ticket rebooking app will be used.
1. Do you use any soccer ticket booking technologies? Can you walk me through the technologies you often use and tell me what are the features of the technologies that you use the most? And show me the above stated features?
2. Did you run into any troubles when using those technologies? What were they?
3. Do you have any expectations of technologies for booking soccer tickets?

Users

I interviewed 7 people. They all had experiences of going to soccer matches. Their ages ranged between 19 to 30.

  • Interview Results

    Most users were concerned about the speed and conviniency of getting compensation for their loss. They told me the pains of dealing with customer representatives to transfer tickets or having a hard time to find a match that they could transfer for.

  • Users' Need

    They were fine with either getting a refund or transferring their tickets for another game as a way to compensate for their loss. But they do want the rebooking process to be simple and fast.

Create affinity diagram.
Purpose: organize activity notes into common patterns and themes across users.
Create Personas (main and secondary).
Goal: to create reliable and realistic representations of the key audience segments for reference.
Identify pain points:
1. Users can't easily find another match they could transfer their tickets for.
2. Users don't really want to transfer their tickets because they wanted to go to the original game to support specific leagues.
3. If a user selects VIP seat in one game, he would like to choose a VIP seat for the next game. Same thing with people who choose average seats.
STEP2. THINK

Brainstorm ideas based on the personas I created.

STEP3. DESIGN

Low-fi Prototype: Paper Prototype
I made a paper prototype and tested with users to see if it is intuitive to use.

Main Features
1. Users can search a match by leagues and then transfer their tickets.
I made this design choice to satisfy users' need of supporting their favorite leagues. Some interviewees said that transferring to another game with no leagues they were interested in was meaningless to them. Searching a game by leagues would quickly help them find the matches they would want to transfer for.


2. Users can search a match by date and then transfer their tickets.
Some users had trouble transferring their tickets for other games because the game schedules conflicted with their schedules. With Ticket Master, a user can search a game by dates to quickly find out if a game schedule could fit in his schedule. The dropdown calendar shows the date and time of the matches and disables users to select dates without any matches. This calendar feature saves users much time for browsing matches.

AB Testing
I did AB Testing on some interaction designs such as the interaction of choosing search by league or date. To choose a way to search, users could either tap on a tab or clicked on a dropdown menu. The test result was that most users preferred the tab design as it was more simple than the dropdown menu.

Hi-Fi Prototype
I also used InVision to prototype my design.

STEP4. TEST

User Testing
I asked participants to perform some tasks using the InVision prototype. Here's the prompt I gave to participants:
1. There is a small earthquake, and a soccer game you’re attending is cancelled just as you arrive at the stadium. Please note that for this test, you’ll be using a prototype, not a finished product. Some features may be missing or unresponsive. If something doesn’t work as expected, tell us what you think should happen.
2. Please use this app to transfer only 1 ticket for another game. What can you do here, what's it for, what strikes you about it?
3. What, if anything, do you like or dislike? Since this isn’t a finished design, what would you like to see in the final version?
Heuristics Evaluation
I did Heuristic evaluation based on users' feedback to understand how to improve the design.

STEP5. IMPROVE
Based on the Heuristic evaluation, I learned that most the interaction of selecting seats needed to be improved. Users thought that if would be inconvinient if they had to check how much each seat was and then chose seats with prices similar to the seats in the cancelled game. So I changed the design of how users could select seats. Instead of telling users which section was not selected by others yet, I labeled sections which had available seats with prices similar to the seats in the cancelled game. I changed the legend wording "available" to "Transferrable Seats".
The design helps users to quickly find seats that they could transfer to.

Coupon
Also, to compensate their loss of not being able to go to the original game, every user is granted with a $10 coupon.
